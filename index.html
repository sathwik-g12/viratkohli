1. EARLY LIFE AND CHILDHOOD

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, into a Punjabi Hindu family. His father, Prem Kohli, was a criminal lawyer, and his mother, Saroj Kohli, is a housewife. From a young age, Virat showed a keen interest in cricket, often wielding a bat and asking his father to bowl to him. He attended Vishal Bharti Public School, where he was noted for his academic prowess as well as his sportsmanship. His family recognized his talent and enrolled him in the West Delhi Cricket Academy at the age of nine.Tragically, his father passed away in 2006, but this event only strengthened his resolve to succeed in cricket. Despite the emotional setback, Kohli continued to work hard and focus on his game, driven by the desire to fulfill his father's dream.

2. INTRODUCTION TO CRICKET

Virat Kohli’s journey in cricket began at the West Delhi Cricket Academy, where he was coached by Rajkumar Sharma. His rigorous training and natural talent quickly made him a standout player among his peers. Kohli represented Delhi in various age-group tournaments, showcasing his skill and maturity. His breakthrough came during the 2006-07 season when he scored 757 runs in the Vijay Merchant Trophy, India’s national level competition for U-17 cricketers. His performances earned him a spot in the India U-19 team, where he continued to impress with his aggressive batting and leadership qualities. This early success laid the foundation for his future career and set him on a path to international stardom.

3. DOMESTIC CAREER AND RISE TO PROMINENCE

Virat Kohli’s domestic career began with Delhi in the Ranji Trophy, India’s premier first-class cricket competition. His most memorable performance came in December 2006, when he scored 90 runs against Karnataka, playing despite the recent loss of his father. This innings showcased his mental strength and dedication. Over the next few years, he consistently scored runs for Delhi, cementing his reputation as a promising young talent. His performances in the domestic circuit, particularly in the 2008-09 season, caught the attention of national selectors. Kohli’s ability to perform under pressure and his leadership skills were further highlighted when he captained the India U-19 team to victory in the 2008 ICC U-19 World Cup.

4. INTERNATIONAL DEBUT

Virat Kohli made his international debut for India in an ODI against Sri Lanka on August 18, 2008. Opening the batting in the absence of senior players, he scored a modest 12 runs. Despite a slow start to his international career, Kohli’s potential was evident, and he soon became a regular in the ODI squad. His breakthrough performance came in 2009, during India’s tour of Sri Lanka, where he scored his maiden ODI century. This marked the beginning of a prolific career as he established himself as a mainstay in the Indian batting lineup. Kohli’s aggressive style, coupled with his ability to chase down targets, quickly made him a fan favorite and a vital asset to the team.

5. ODI AND T20 SUCCESS

Virat Kohli’s success in ODIs and T20s is marked by his extraordinary consistency and ability to perform in high-pressure situations. He has set numerous records, including being the fastest player to reach 8,000, 9,000, and 10,000 runs in ODIs. Kohli’s knack for chasing down targets earned him the moniker “Chase Master.” In T20 Internationals, his quick scoring and aggressive style have made him one of the most feared batsmen in the format. He played a crucial role in India’s triumph in the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy. Kohli’s leadership in the shorter formats, combined with his batting prowess, has solidified his reputation as one of the greatest limited-overs cricketers of all time.

6. TEST CRICKET ACHIVEMENTS

Virat Kohli’s achievements in Test cricket are a testament to his versatility and adaptability. He made his Test debut in June 2011 against the West Indies. Initially, he faced challenges adapting to the longer format, but his determination saw him improve steadily. Kohli’s breakthrough in Tests came during India’s tour of Australia in 2014-15, where he scored four centuries in the series. As a Test batsman, he has set numerous records, including being the fastest Indian to reach 1,000 runs in Test cricket. Under his captaincy, India achieved the No. 1 ranking in Tests and registered historic series wins abroad, including a series win in Australia in 2018-19. Kohli’s aggressive and fitness-oriented approach has transformed the Indian Test team into a formidable unit.

7. CAPTAINCY ERA

Virat Kohli took over as India’s Test captain in 2014 and later became the captain in all formats in 2017. His captaincy era is marked by a focus on fitness, aggression, and a winning mentality. Under his leadership, India became the No. 1 ranked Test team and achieved significant victories, including a historic series win in Australia in 2018-19. Kohli is known for his proactive and bold decision-making on the field. He led India to the final of the ICC Champions Trophy in 2017 and the semi-final of the 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup. Despite some criticisms, Kohli’s captaincy has been widely praised for transforming India into a more competitive and aggressive side across all formats.

8. NOTABLE PERFORMANCES AND RECORDS

Virat Kohli’s career is studded with notable performances and records. He holds the record for the fastest century by an Indian in ODIs, achieved in just 52 balls against Australia in 2013. Kohli has the most centuries in run-chases in ODIs, underscoring his ability to perform under pressure. He is the fastest player to reach 10,000 runs in ODIs, achieving the milestone in 205 innings.In Test cricket, he has numerous double centuries to his name and is one of the few players to score centuries in all formats of the game. Kohli’s consistent performances across different conditions and against various opponents have solidified his place among cricket’s all-time greats.

9. INJURIES AND COMEBACKS

Throughout his career, Virat Kohli has faced several injuries but has always managed to make strong comebacks. Injuries, including a shoulder injury during the 2017 Australia series, have occasionally sidelined him, but his rigorous fitness regime has helped in quick recoveries. Kohli’s resilience and determination are evident in his ability to return to form swiftly after injuries. His commitment to maintaining peak physical condition has not only aided his recovery but also set new fitness standards for his teammates. Kohli’s ability to bounce back from setbacks has been a hallmark of his career, showcasing his mental toughness and dedication to the sport.

10. OFF-FIELD CONTRIBUTIONS AND PHILANTHROPY

Off the field, Virat Kohli is known for his philanthropic efforts and contributions to society. He established the Virat Kohli Foundation, which focuses on supporting underprivileged children and promoting sports. The foundation organizes charity events and collaborates with various NGOs to provide education, healthcare, and sports opportunities to children. Kohli has also been involved in initiatives related to animal welfare and disaster relief. His efforts extend to promoting fitness and a healthy lifestyle, inspiring many with his own fitness journey. Kohli’s off-field contributions reflect his desire to give back to the community and make a positive impact beyond cricket.

11. PERSONAL LIFE AND FAMILY

Virat Kohli married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma on December 11, 2017, in a private ceremony in Italy. The couple is one of the most popular and influential power couples in India. They welcomed their daughter, Vamika, in January 2021. Kohli’s personal life has often been in the limelight, but he has managed to maintain a balance between his professional and personal commitments. His relationship with Anushka has been a source of support and stability for him. Despite the pressures of fame, Kohli remains grounded and focuses on his family’s privacy and well-being. His family has been a pillar of strength throughout his career.

12. BUISNESS VENTURES AND ENDORSEMENTS

Virat Kohli is not only a cricketing icon but also a successful entrepreneur and one of the highest-earning athletes in the world. He has numerous endorsement deals with major brands such as Puma, Audi, and MRF, making him one of the most marketable athletes globally. Kohli co-owns the fashion brand WROGN and has invested in various startups, including the fitness chain Chisel and the Indian Super League football club FC Goa. His business ventures reflect his keen interest in fashion, fitness, and sports. Kohli’s savvy business acumen and brand endorsements have significantly contributed to his status as a global sports icon.

13. FITNESS REGIME AND DIET

Virat Kohli’s transformation into one of the fittest athletes in the world is well-documented. His rigorous fitness regime includes weight training, cardio exercises, and specific routines designed to enhance agility and endurance. Kohli follows a strict diet plan, focusing on high protein intake, balanced with healthy fats and carbohydrates. He avoids junk food and maintains a disciplined lifestyle, often sharing fitness tips and routines with his fans.His dedication to fitness has not only improved his performance on the field but also inspired many aspiring athletes to prioritize their health and fitness. Kohli’s emphasis on fitness has set new standards in Indian cricket.

14. CONTROVERSIES AND CRITICISMS

Like many high-profile athletes, Virat Kohli’s career has not been without controversies and criticisms. He has been involved in on-field spats with players and has occasionally been criticized for his aggressive behavior and confrontational attitude. Kohli’s decision-making as a captain has also faced scrutiny, particularly during key matches where his strategies were questioned. Despite these controversies, Kohli has often responded with stellar performances on the field. He has acknowledged his mistakes and worked on improving his conduct, striving to balance his competitive spirit with sportsmanship. Kohli’s ability to learn from criticism and evolve as a player and leader has been a crucial aspect of his journey.

15. ROYAL CHALLENGERS BENGALURU (RCB) Career

Virat Kohli’s career with the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) in the Indian Premier League (IPL) has been one of dedication and remarkable performances. Joining RCB in 2008 as a young talent, Kohli quickly rose through the ranks to become the team’s captain in 2013. Under his leadership, he has consistently been one of the top run-scorers in the league, including a record-breaking season in 2016 where he scored 973 runs, the highest in a single IPL season. Despite RCB’s fluctuating fortunes in the tournament, Kohli’s commitment to the team and his ability to inspire and lead from the front have made him a central figure in the franchise’s history. His loyalty to RCB and his never-give-up attitude have endeared him to fans.

16. LEGACY AND IMPACT ON CRICKET

Virat Kohli’s legacy in cricket is defined by his extraordinary achievements and the impact he has had on the game. As one of the greatest batsmen of his generation, his records and milestones across all formats speak volumes. Kohli’s aggressive and fitness-oriented approach has revolutionized the Indian cricket team, setting new standards for performance and professionalism. His influence extends beyond his playing career, inspiring a new generation of cricketers with his work ethic, dedication, and passion for the game. Kohli’s contributions to Indian cricket and his global influence have cemented his place as a cricketing legend whose impact will be felt for years to come.
